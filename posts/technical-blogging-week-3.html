Why Technical Blogging Matters in IT (Week 3 Reflection)

This reflection explains how technical blogging supports learning, documentation, professional identity, and future IT career preparation.

Hello everyone,

This week in my IT138 course, I learned that building a technical blog is not just an assignment. It is an important step in developing a professional identity in the IT field.

Background

After creating my first blog using GitHub Pages, this week I focused on understanding why blogging is important for networking students and IT professionals.

Problem

At first, I was not sure what kind of content I should write or how blogging could help my career. I also wondered if my weekly learning was important enough to share publicly.

What I Learned

From this week's lesson, I learned that a technical blog helps in several important ways:

  • It builds a public portfolio that shows real skills.
  • It improves technical understanding by explaining concepts.
  • It helps students stand out in a competitive job market.
  • It allows sharing knowledge and helping others.

Solution / Approach

I decided to follow a simple structure for my blog posts:

  • Objective: What I am trying to learn.
  • Hurdle: What problem I faced.
  • Solution: How I solved it.

This structure makes my posts more clear and useful.

Real-World Connection

In real IT jobs, documentation is very important. Blogging helps me practice writing technical explanations, which is useful for roles like system administrator, cloud engineer, and IT support.

What I Will Do Next

Going forward, I will continue writing about:

  • Linux commands and labs.
  • AWS cloud concepts.
  • Cybersecurity topics.
  • Troubleshooting experiences.

Conclusion

This week helped me understand that blogging is not just writing. It is a way to learn faster, build a portfolio, and prepare for my future IT career.

Thank you for reading!
