Understanding the Importance of Technical Blogging (Week 3)

A Week 3 IT138 reflection about how technical blogging supports learning, documentation, professional identity, and future IT career preparation.

Hello everyone,

This week in my IT138 class, I learned more about why creating a technical blog is important for IT and cybersecurity students.

Background

After successfully creating my blog using GitHub Pages last week, this week I focused on understanding the real purpose behind blogging. At first, I thought this was just a class requirement, but I realized it has a much bigger impact on my future career.

Problem

I was not sure what kind of content I should write in my blog. I also questioned if my weekly learning was useful enough to share publicly. It was a bit confusing to understand how a simple blog could help me as an IT student.

What I Learned

From this week's lesson, I learned that a technical blog is very useful for several reasons:

  • It helps build a professional portfolio that I can share with employers.
  • It improves my understanding because writing helps me learn better.
  • It allows me to document problems and solutions for future reference.
  • It helps me stand out from other students.

Solution / Approach

To make my blog more useful, I decided to follow a simple structure for each post:

  • Background: What I am working on.
  • Problem: What issue I faced.
  • Solution: How I solved it.
  • What I Learned: Key takeaways.

This approach makes my blog more organized and easier to understand.

Real-World Connection

In real IT jobs, documentation is very important. System administrators, cloud engineers, and IT support specialists all need to document their work. Blogging helps me practice this skill early.

What I Will Do Next

Going forward, I will continue writing about:

  • Linux commands and labs.
  • AWS cloud concepts.
  • Cybersecurity topics.
  • Troubleshooting experiences.

Conclusion

This week helped me understand that blogging is not just an assignment. It is a way to improve my skills, build a professional profile, and prepare for my future career in IT.

Thank you for reading!
